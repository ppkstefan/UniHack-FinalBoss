English Vocabulary Exercise 3

1. The teacher's explanation was clear/confusing/complicated, making the topic easy to understand.

2. The marathon runner felt exhausted/energized/relaxed after finishing the race.

3. The artist's work was considered inspiring/boring/ordinary by the critics.

4. The new restaurant in town is popular/empty/unknown, attracting many visitors every day.

5. The weather forecast predicted it would be sunny/rainy/stormy tomorrow.

6. The team played with enthusiasm/laziness/indifference throughout the match.

7. The book was so engaging/boring/tedious that I couldn't put it down.

8. The dessert was delicious/bland/average, making it a crowd favorite.

9. The CEO's speech was motivational/boring/unclear, leaving the employees inspired.

10. The puzzle was challenging/easy/boring but satisfying to complete.

11. The experiment results were unexpected/predictable/unclear to the researchers.

12. The comedian's joke was hilarious/boring/awkward, making the audience laugh loudly.

13. The lake was calm/stormy/muddy on that beautiful summer day.

14. The concert was amazing/disappointing/average, and the crowd loved it.

15. The movie's ending was unexpected/predictable/cliff-hanger.

16. The dog's behavior was friendly/aggressive/scared when meeting new people.

17. The painting was described as colorful/dark/plain by the art critic.

18. The city's traffic was chaotic/calm/slow during the rush hour.

19. The new gadget was innovative/useless/expensive according to the reviews.

20. The dessert was too sweet/bland/perfect according to the guests.

21. The party was lively/boring/quiet, with people dancing all night.

22. The lecture was informative/dull/complicated, helping students understand the topic.

23. The garden was beautiful/overgrown/neglected in the spring.

24. The athlete was determined/tired/discouraged to win the race.

25. The mystery novel was thrilling/predictable/boring until the last chapter.
